About Troy Public Library

Over 225 years of serving our community

Troy Public Library has been a cornerstone of our community since 1799, making it one of the oldest cultural institutions in Troy, New York.

The library was chartered in 1905 to serve the City of Troy after the union of the Troy Lending Library, the Troy Young Men's Association, and the Free Reading Room of Troy. Since 1897, the library's home has been the magnificent Hart Memorial Building on Second Street in historic downtown Troy.

As a special district library established by New York State in 2010, we proudly serve the Troy community with two locations, extensive collections, innovative programming, and a commitment to free and open access to information for all.

Our History

From a letter to the editor in 1799 to a modern library system serving thousands

1799
An anonymous "Citizen of Troy" wrote to the Troy Northern Budget on November 13, 1799, appealing for the establishment of a public library to provide "a correction of morals and a source of mental improvement" for the people of Troy.
1800
A collection of books became available for circulation, and a group of stockholders formed the Troy Library.
1835
The Troy Young Men's Association was incorporated, and the library joined forces with the organization to seek a permanent location.
1845-1846
The Troy Library voted to disband and turned its property over to the Troy Young Men's Association. The collection moved to the Athenaeum Building.
1859
The first complete catalog was produced, listing 12,067 volumes. Several copies of this catalog still exist today.
1869
The library became a Federal Government Depository Library, a status it maintains to this day.
1877
The Free Reading Room of Troy was incorporated with the goal of providing free library service to all citizens of Troy.
1879
The Young Men's Association and the Free Reading Room merged in what they called "The Public Library Enterprise."
1896-1897
Mary E. Hart donated funds to construct the Hart Memorial Building in honor of her late husband, William Howard Hart. Designed by Barney and Chapman of New York City, the building is recognized as one of the finest examples of American Renaissance architecture in the country.
1905
Troy Public Library was officially chartered to serve the City of Troy.
1980
The Lansingburgh Branch officially opened on November 22, 1980.
2010
New York State established the City of Troy as a special district for the Library, allowing for a dedicated budget and increased community control.

The Hart Memorial Building

The Hart Memorial Building, completed in 1897, is an architectural masterpiece and one of the finest examples of American Renaissance style in the United States.

Designed by the prestigious New York City architectural firm of Barney and Chapman, the building is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and is a contributing property to the Central Troy Historic District.

Architectural Highlights

  • Exterior walls constructed of white Vermont marble
  • Rusticated first story contrasted with dressed Ashlar masonry on the upper story
  • Five-bay loggia at the second story level on Ferry Street side
  • Original Tiffany stained glass window depicting master printer Aldus Manutius printing Dante's Divine Comedy
  • Highly detailed carved stone courses and ornamental windows
  • Stone parapet with balustrade over the entrance

The Troy Room: Local History & Special Collections

The Troy Room at Troy Public Library houses the library's local history and special collections. The collection contains a wealth of resources for genealogical and historical research on the City of Troy, Rensselaer County, and the greater Capital Region area.

Included in the collection are census records, city directories, indexes of vital records, historic newspapers, photographs, scrapbooks, and much more. Many of these materials are being digitized and made available through New York Heritage.
